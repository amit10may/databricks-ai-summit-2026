## 🏋️ Big-number flexes (the "we're not like other vendors" energy)

- **"1+ quadrillion tokens per year."** Agent Bricks now processes a *quadrillion* tokens annually,
  with 100k+ agents built. When your unit of measure is "quadrillion," subtlety has left the room.
  *(Agent Bricks blog)*
- **"84.5% vs 52.4% (and the weakest only 25%)."** Genie Ontology's internal benchmark vs
  "the strongest general-purpose coding agent" — *and* 2× faster. This is the data-nerd equivalent
  of posting your gym PR with the other guy's numbers in the same photo. *(Genie blog)*
- **Lakebase is growing "more than twice the rate" of their own data-warehousing product.** A flex
  so confident it casually dunks on Databricks' *own* warehouse. *(Lakebase coverage)*
- **$4B revenue run-rate, $1B+ of it AI.** The "we'll be fine, thanks" slide. *(Databricks newsroom)*

## 🥷 Competitor subtweets (no names, but we all know)

- **"Open over locked-in."** Matei Zaharia's framing of OpenSharing, *as paraphrased by Tech Times*:
  Delta Sharing proved the industry would choose open over locked-in, and OpenSharing extends that
  bet. The phrase "locked-in" is doing Olympic-level subtweeting at you-know-who. *(Tech Times)*
- **"…without relying on proprietary marketplaces."** OpenSharing's pitch, repeated like a mantra —
  later framed as preventing any single platform from "controlling the exchange layer… the way
  proprietary app stores control software distribution." Shots fired at the app-store economy.
  *(UC blog / Tech Times)*
- **"Hard spend caps stop requests when a budget is reached, rather than just alerting after the
  fact."** Translation: *other tools wait until you're broke to tell you.* A clean little jab baked
  into a feature bullet. *(Unity AI Gateway blog)*
- **Document Intelligence: "highest quality and lowest cost compared to both frontier LLMs and also
  specialized systems from other providers."** The rare double-dunk — beats the big general models
  *and* the boutique specialists. *(Agent Bricks blog)*

## 🤡 Spicy ironies (best enjoyed with popcorn)

- **They partner with everyone… then beat them on a slide.** Agent Bricks adds OpenAI, Anthropic,
  Gemini, Qwen, Kimi, and Grok — then notes a Databricks custom model is "competitive with frontier
  models such as Opus and Sonnet… while being significantly lower cost per query." Come for the
  partnership, stay for the benchmark. *(Agent Bricks blog)*
- **A self-roast of the entire agent industry:** the platform's pitch literally cites that "the news
  is replete with agents accidentally deleting codebases, or prompt-injecting to leaking valuable
  information." Nothing sells governance like reminding everyone agents are one bad prompt from
  chaos. *(Agent Bricks blog)*

## 🧠 Meme-able coinages (screenshot bait)

- **"Tokenmaxing."** Officially the MVP word of the summit: employees "'tokenmaxing' their agentic
  coding leaderboards." It's "looksmaxing" for people who burn GPU budget to top the internal Cursor
  scoreboard. Put it on a mug. *(Agent Bricks blog)*
- **"A Cambrian explosion of agents."** The biology-flex way of saying "there are SO many agents
  now." *(Agent Bricks blog)*
- **"The core agent loop is just 1% of the work. The other 99% is the hidden technical debt."**
  The whole keynote thesis in one tweet-length line. Expect this on a lot of LinkedIn carousels.
  *(Agent Bricks blog)*

## 🆕 Fresh from Days 3–4 (sync 2026-06-19)

- **"Every cron schedule is a guess at when data is ready."** Lakeflow Jobs' pitch for data-readiness
  triggers — a tidy little funeral for the 3 a.m. cron job. *(Lakeflow blog)*
- **External Orchestration "can trigger Snowflake jobs."** Databricks shipped a feature whose example
  use case is… running your rival's jobs from inside Databricks. Bold flex, lightly petty. *(Lakeflow blog)*
- **"No separate Flink engine."** Real-Time Mode for Spark Declarative Pipelines hits ~5 ms latency
  while pointedly noting you no longer need to babysit a second streaming engine. Flink left on read.
  *(Lakeflow blog)*
- **"Consolidating legacy orchestrators, such as Apache Airflow."** Airflow named directly as the thing
  you're meant to retire. *(Lakeflow blog)*
- **"300K+ QPS at under 10 ms p99… no knobs to tune."** Model Serving flex, with a side of "cut
  infrastructure costs by up to 90%+ vs self-managed stacks." The "90% cheaper than doing it yourself"
  slide never misses. *(AI Platform blog)*

## 🎤 Bold claims (the "frame it on the wall" tier)

- **"The catalog has moved from a system of record to a runtime decision-maker for AI."** Translation:
  the boring metadata layer is now the main character. *(Unity Catalog blog)*
- **Genie "doesn't trade off accuracy for speed."** The classic "you can have it all" line — backed,
  to be fair, by an actual benchmark. *(Genie blog)*
- **OpenSharing as "the first open, vendor-neutral protocol for securely sharing AI assets."**
  Planting a flag on the entire agentic-economy exchange layer. Ambitious. *(UC blog)*
